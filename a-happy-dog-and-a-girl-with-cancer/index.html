A Happy Dog and a Girl with cancer

In this blog, I am sharing one of my experiences that made me question my outlook on life. This happened at a cancer hospital when I was accompanying my grandmother at the hospital.

Sathish Kannan M

Jul 9, 2021 • 4 min read

In this blog, I am sharing one of my experiences that made me question my outlook on life. This happened at a cancer hospital when I was accompanying my grandmother at the hospital.

Let's move on.

The following happened at the end of January 2020. My Grandmother had Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma. She is well now and recovered from cancer. At that time, she was undergoing chemotherapy at Adyar Cancer Institute, Chennai.

Since my mother and father were working and I was free. I just accompanied my Grandmother to fix the date for chemotherapy and take some tests before that.
We came to the hospital at 9 am. There was a place with chairs for people to wait. It's like a waiting area, but it was almost open. We had a roof, but anybody can pass through that area.

In the afternoon, my Grandmother went to take a test. I decided to have my lunch.
Suddenly, a street dog approached me. The street dog was not like a stereotypical street dog depicted in movies, the lean and dirty. This dog was clean and healthy. I thought it is looking for my food. But I ignored it, thinking that it would go. But it didn't and tried to shoo it away. But it didn't move. To add some background, I am afraid of dogs. Even now, I would try to avoid dogs as much as possible, and I am 22 years old.

Continuing, the dog didn't move from me. It just kept looking at me. I thought it would try to bark and jump to get food if I kept ignoring it. So, I moved to a new seat far from there. It followed me there.

By this time, my Grandmother me in the waiting area. She started to eat with me. She tried to shoo the dog away, but it didn't go away. Then she decided to give it some food. The food was prawn curry and rice. She went to the back of the waiting area. It had sand and stones. She put some food in a rock. The dog didn't eat. I was confused. What does the dog want? Maybe the dog didn't like prawns. It reminded me of the dogs in Madras Christian College. The dogs never eat rice, even if we give them. They will only eat if we provide them with meat. I know that from my personal experience.

So, the dog didn't eat. But it came to us again. I can't understand. It didn't like our food but keep on coming to us.

It wandered around us for another 15 minutes. After that, it moved to a young woman in a red chudithar with her mom. She was covering her head with a scarf. Her head was shaved. Most probably, She could be undergoing chemotherapy.
The dog did the same thing. It didn't move and kept looking at her. The girl saw the dog and said "hey" and brushed the dogs head and neck with her hand. She did that for five minutes while talking to her mom occasionally.

The dog sat beside her and laid on the ground, and went to blissful sleep. I could see that from the way it was relaxed and happy while sleeping.

I realised I was too dumb, and It just wanted love. I could have never guessed it.

This incident touched my heart. The girl would have been going through pain physically and emotionally due to cancer treatment. But she was not sad and showed love without hesitation.

The scene which I experienced created a conflict in me. I asked myself when was the last time I sought love and gave love to anything or anybody like that. Of course, my parents love me and love them. But the love I saw was blissful. The dog was blissful.

I was so fearful of the dog, but it never meant any harm to me.
The dog life was simple. It searches for food. I am pretty sure that the dog is getting enough food as it was very healthy. After food, it is searching for love. That's it. It never searches for anything else. It lives a content life with a content heart, not worrying about any other things in life. The dog was happy.

What about my life? I have dreams; I am working for that. I am not perfect in that. Sometimes, I think I want to prove myself. But do I have a content heart? Am I happy? Am I blissful?

The dog never has any dreams, and it is blissful. Why I am not blissful?
Our dreams and ambitions a curse?
What should I do to be content and happy?
I don't know. But I know I am seeking answers.

Conclusion

What is the roadway to be content? Is being content important? Does happiness really matter? Yes, I think being content and happy is important. But what do we do for that? Does it really make us happy? A street dog can be content and happy with food and love. What does a human need to be content and happy?

What do you think the way to be content and happy? Leave it as comments.
